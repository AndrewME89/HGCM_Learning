Back-to-Back (B2B) Bookings

A Back-to-Back (B2B) booking occurs when a guest has two consecutive reservations for an extended stay.

Check-In Procedure
  1. Print the registration card for the second booking and have the guest complete and sign both cards for their entire stay.

  2. Process payment for both reservations upon arrival. Mark the second registration card with "B2B" in the top right corner.

  3. Ensure both bookings in iHMS are assigned to the same room.

    • If different room types were booked, explain the room move procedure to the guest.

    • If the guest prefers to stay in the same room:

      • Upgrade the room type and charge the applicable fee.

      • Downgrade the room type and inform the guest that the rate cannot be adjusted.

      • Consult the Duty Manager if unsure.

  4. Cut keys for the guest's entire stay and note "B2B" in the remarks section.

  5. Set a task for the Night Audit to roll the reservation on the appropriate date.

  6. Staple both registration cards together and file them in the in-house guest bin.

Actioning B2B Bookings (Night Audit)

The Night Auditor is responsible for completing the B2B process on the correct date.

  1. Check for outstanding charges on the original (first) reservation.

    • If there are no outstanding charges, check out the old reservation and check-in the new one.

    • If there are outstanding charges, check the new reservation into a Virtual Folio:

      1. Right-click the folio and select "Check-In."

      2. Change the "Room" dropdown to "Virtual" and complete the check-in process.

      3. Open the original reservation's folio and transfer any outstanding charges to the new booking:

        1. Go to the "Transactions" tab and highlight the charges to transfer.

        2. Right-click, select "Transfer," and choose the Virtual Room in the "To Folio" field. Click "OK."

  2. Ensure no charges remain on the original booking and then check it out.

  3. Return to the Virtual Folio and change the room to the guest's assigned room.

  4. Update the folio, copying any important remarks (e.g., LCO, Pre-Auth ID).

Handling Stay Extensions
  • Direct Bookings: Extend the dates in the existing reservation.

  • Third-Party Bookings: Create a new reservation for the extended period.

  • Print the new registration card with updated dates, have the guest sign it, and process any additional payments (e.g., top up pre-authorisation). Provide the guest with new or re-cut keys.
