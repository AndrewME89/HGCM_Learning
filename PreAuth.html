Pre Authorisations

Upon check in, the Hotel will require to pre-authorise $100 on a guest’s credit card.

These funds are a security bond and guarantee for any incidentals, damages, or undeclared charges (such as F&B or car parking etc).

A Preauthorisation is a pending transaction and gives the hotel authorisation to use those funds for any outstanding charges.

However, certain banking institutions such as NAB, WESTPAC and etc may show the pre-authorisation as successfully transacted rather than pending.

Hotel reserves the right to increase the pre-authorisation if deemed necessary to guarantee any additional charges.

Every guest MUST provide security bond on for each stay. Some VIP clients or those with an appropriate charge back reflected in check in instruction in HMS (All Charge Back [ACB] or Z [bond] may not be required to provide a bond. However, Duty Managers have the right to waive or amend the amount at their discretions.

Pre – Authorisation amount

Guest must present a physical credit which needs be inserted into EFTPOS terminal machine in order for the team member to process the pre-authorisation.

The pre-authorisation amount is $100 per room per stay.

Cash OR EFTPOS or Tapping on the phone *Guest must know their pin number to process refund on check out*

  • Cash - $300 per room per stay

  • EFTPOS card - $300 per room per stay

  • Guest tapping their cards using phones or smart watch - $300 per room per stay

Explaining pre-authorisation to guests.
In the event of no charge and the whole amount has been released:

Your Bond payment will be cancelled and released today.

Please be aware that it may take 5 - 7 business days for the funds to be available, depending on your bank

You may see the transaction on your bank statement while the reversal is being processed, though this will disappear once the reversal has been finalised.

Please note that this amount will not show up as a refund, it will simply no longer be visible on your account.

If there are Incidentals to be charged:

Due to there being Incidentals associated with your account, we can utilise your bond to cover the owed amount, while the remainder will be automatically released to your account.

Please be aware that it may take 5 - 7 business days for the funds to be available, depending on your bank

Please note that this amount will not show up as a refund, it will simply no longer be visible on your account.

If there are Incidentals and a Top Up has occurred:

As the total cost for your Incidentals has exceeded your initial deposit of $100, your Pre Authorisation has been Topped Up accordingly.

As such, your current bond amount is sufficient to cover the Incidentals for your stay.

Please be aware that it may take 5 - 7 business days for the funds to be available, depending on your bank

Please note that this amount will not show up as a refund, it will simply no longer be visible on your account.

Processing standard Pre-Authorisation.
  1. Press Menu on the touch screen OR the green arrow button on EFTPOS machine.

  2. Choose the ‘Pre-auth’ option.

  3. Choose ‘pre-auth’ option again.

  4. Key in $100 or total pre-auth needed and press the green arrow button

  5. Insert guest’s credit or debit card into the machine

  6. Choose credit or debit option. Pre-authorisation cannot be processed using cheque or saving account

  7. Request guest to enter in the pin code and press the green arrow button.

  8. Once approved, removed the card from the machine.

  9. Print out 2 copies of the terminal dockets.

    • One copy to be attached at the back of the registration card

    • Second copy to be attached on the tax invoice along with the pre-authorisation explanation and given to the guest.

  10. Update pre-auth information in HMS.

    • In (1) Corporate tab, change the payment status deposit paid to Pre-authorisation obtained.

    • In (1) Corporate tab, then enter the deposit amount taken. (i.e: $100 / $200, etc..)

    • In (6) Communication tab, in Check out instruction box, enter the pre-auth id found on the docket.

Completing Pre-Authorisation.

At the time of check out the final charge will be processed from the pre-authorised amount to settle your bill. Only the final charge will appear on your credit card statement and any remaining funds will be released back to the account - usually within 3-5 business days (bank dependent). Note that, certain banking institutions can take up, up to 7 business days or longer. International banks may take up to within 2 weeks – 30 days.

Any unused funds will not show as a refund; the pending transaction will be removed from your account. All pre-authorisations that are not used will be cancelled on the day of check out.

This process is the final payment from the amount that has been preauthorised. This can be up to incidentals amount, and including the value of the total amount of the preauthorisation.

  1. Retrieve the pre-auth id from the registration card (Ensure the pre-auth belongs to the correct guest and room)

  2. Press Menu on the touch screen OR the green arrow button on EFTPOS machine.

  3. Choose the ‘Pre-auth’ option from the EFTPOS terminal

  4. Choose ‘Completion’ option

  5. Choose ‘Enter Pre-Auth ID’ option

  6. Enter the pre-auth ID

  7. Details of the pre-auth will appear, confirm the last 4 digits of the credit card match the number of pre-auth docket

  8. Key in the final amount which should be the same from HMS.

  9. Press the green arrow button.

  10. Once approved, print out 2 copies of the terminal dockets.

    • One copy to be attached at the hotel copy of tax invoice

    • Second copy to be attached on the tax invoice to be given to the guest.

  11. Stamp the pre-auth docket on the registration card as ‘Complete’

Any remaining amount from the pre-authorisation will be automatically released back to the guest’s account. However, it may takes up to 3-5 business days (bank dependent)

Cancelling Pre-Authorisation.
Full Cancellation

All pre-authorisations that are not used to pay off any outstanding charges are now required to be cancelled by The Hotel. This process will expedite the release of the funds rather than relying on the banking institution to release these.

  1. Retrieve the pre-auth id from the registration card (Ensure the pre-auth belongs to the correct guest and room)

  2. Press Menu on the touch screen OR the green arrow button on EFTPOS machine.

  3. Choose the ‘Pre-auth’ option from the EFTPOS terminal

  4. Choose ‘Cancellation’ option

  5. Choose ‘Enter Pre-Auth ID’ option

  6. Enter the pre-auth ID and press the green arrow button

  7. Details of the pre-auth will appear, confirm the last 4 digits of the credit card match the number of pre-auth docket.

  8. Confirm by pressing the green arrow button

  9. Print out the cancellation docket and attached to the registration card

Partial Cancellation

This will function will partially reduce funds held on the original pre-authorisation.

Please seek manager approval if a partial cancellation is required or requested. A Partial Cancelation may be processed for a card in the event of certain funds being confirmed to be covered via a different payment method or an excess amount was incorrectly processed on arrival.

  1. Retrieve the pre-auth id from the registration card (Ensure the pre-auth belongs to the correct guest and room)

  2. Press Menu on the touch screen OR the green arrow button on EFTPOS machine.

  3. Choose the ‘Pre-auth’ option from the EFTPOS terminal

  4. Press the arrow down icon on the screen to go the second page

  5. Choose ‘partial cancellation’ option

  6. Choose ‘Enter Pre-Auth ID’ option

  7. Enter the pre-auth ID

  8. Details of the pre-auth will appear, confirm the last 4 digits of the credit card match the number of pre-auth docket.

  9. Confirm by pressing the green arrow button

  10. Enter the amount that needs to be cancelled and press the green arrow button.

  11. Print out the cancellation docket and attached to the registration card

Note:

AMEX cards cannot be cancelled using the terminal. Please contact AMEX’s customer service centre to cancel the pre-authorisation.

Topping Up Pre Authorisation

This function allows The Hotel to increase the holding amount in the event the incidentals are over the current pre-authorisation amount.

Any incidental above the current amount will be topped up by night team once the credit check is completed.

  1. Retrieve the pre-auth id from the registration card (Ensure the pre-auth belongs to the correct guest and room)

  2. Press Menu on the touch screen OR the green arrow button on EFTPOS machine.

  3. Choose the ‘Pre-auth’ option from the EFTPOS terminal

  4. Press the arrow down icon on the screen to go the second page

  5. Choose ‘Top Up’ option

  6. Choose ‘Enter Pre-Auth ID’ option

  7. Enter the pre-auth ID

  8. Details of the pre-auth will appear, confirm the last 4 digits of the credit card match the number of pre-auth docket.

  9. Confirm by pressing the green arrow

  10. Enter the amount to be topped up and press green arrow button to confirm

  11. Print out the docket and attach to the registration card besides the original docket.

  12. Update the new amount in HMS. In (1) Corporate tab, then enter new deposit amount taken. (i.e: $220, etc..)

Note:

AMEX and UnionPay cards cannot be topped using the terminal.

Extending a Pre Authorisation

All Pre-Authorisations have an expiration date, these are usually between 5 and 30 days, depending on the banking institution that particular account is with.

In the event of a Pre - Authorisation is pending for a long period of time, an extension will be required to be processed through the terminal.

Pre-Authorisations may expire if they have been processed in advance of a booking/or for a long stay guest/ or in the event that the guest has left and we are required to delay processing a final payment.

There may be various reasons for doing advanced or delayed processes - it may be to guarantee a booking ahead of time (during a peak time or special event). There may be damage to a room for which the hotel is waiting on a quote for repairs or maybe even a dispute as to who is covering a particular charge and delaying the completion pending confirmation of payment type.

In any event, when a pre-authorisation is required to be extended follow the following instructions

  1. Retrieve the pre-auth id from the registration card (Ensure the pre-auth belongs to the correct guest and room)

  2. Press Menu on the touch screen OR the green arrow button on EFTPOS machine.

  3. Choose the ‘Pre-auth’ option from the EFTPOS terminal

  4. Press the arrow down icon on the screen to go the second page

  5. Choose ‘Extend’ option

  6. Choose ‘Enter Pre-Auth ID’ option

  7. Enter the pre-auth ID

  8. Select to Extend and enter the relevant times or days

  9. Press the green arrow button.

  10. If the terminal produces a docket, attach this to the Registration Card next to the original Pre- Authorisation
