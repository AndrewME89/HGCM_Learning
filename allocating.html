Allocating Rooms

Strategic room allocation for upcoming arrivals ensures a seamless guest experience and efficient operations. It involves prioritizing certain guest types and considering specific room attributes.

Procedure:
  1. Open Tomorrow's Arrivals
    • In iHMS, go to Reservations â†’ Folio, select "Check In" and enter tomorrow's date in both date fields. Click "Search" to generate a list of arriving guests.

  2. Prioritize Returning Guests
    • Sort the list by "Guest Stay" twice to display guests with the most stays at the top.

    • Allocate rooms to these returning guests first.

    • Note any red "P" symbols indicating guest preferences recorded in their profile.

    • Review the "Special Instructions" tab (6) for any specific requests or notes related to returning guests.

  3. Allocate Rooms
    1. In the "Corporate" tab, click the three dots next to "Unit" in the "Room" box to open the room allocation screen. The default view shows vacant and clean rooms matching the booked room type. Untick the checkboxes to see all available rooms.

    2. Select a room and click "OK." This will return you to the "Corporate" tab.

    3. For returning guests, tick the "Do Not Move" checkbox and enter "DNUBRGA" in the "Remarks" box.

  4. Allocate Remaining Guests
    • After assigning rooms to returning guests, sort the list by "Check Out Date" (twice) to prioritize long-stay guests.

    • Allocate rooms to long-stay guests next.

    • Then, assign rooms to guests with specific requests and tick "Do Not Move."

    • Allocate rooms to groups, placing individuals traveling together on the same floor, and tick "Do Not Move."

    • Leave one-night stays without special requests unassigned.

Allocation Tips:
  • Complimentary Upgrade Priority: Returning Guests, Corporate Guests, High-Rate Guests, Direct Bookings, Industry Guests

  • Assign groups to lower floors.

  • Rooms 02/09 are best for rollaway beds. Rooms 01/10 and 04/07 are less suitable due to furniture layout.

  • Levels 15 and above offer views.

  • Rooms 03/08 are quietest but have a bulkhead in the shower.

  • Rooms 04/05/06/07 on any floor have better views.

  • Levels 10-15 have optimal hot water regulation.

  • Rooms 03/04/05/06/07/08 are away from the lifts.

  • Rooms 01/02/03/08/09/10 are away from the street.
