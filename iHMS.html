Introducing iHMS

Let's take a look at iHMS.

This is what you will see when you first log on:

Rooms

The first element you'll notice upon logging in, is the "Rooms" section.

The Physical Rooms shows how many guest rooms the hotel has.

Inventory Rooms refers to how many rooms are in our inventory.

Out of Order is the rooms which are currently blocked for maintenance or other issues.

Out of Service means that a room is temporarily unavailable. It may be a "Carry" room, or it may require some other attention.

Arrivals

The Arrivals section covers all reservations expected to check in today.

The Groups column shows the incoming groups. In this example, there is one group with two rooms booked, and they have both checked in.

The acronym FIT means Fully Independent Traveller, which refers to people who are not affiliated with a Travel Agency, Company or Group.

In our example, 68 rooms were expected to check in. 19 have already checked in, leaving 49 still to come.

The Total section is the overview of the Arrivals. There were 70 expected check ins, 21 have checked in, and 49 are still to arrive.

Room Status

The Room Status section gives a quick overview of what condition the rooms are in.

In this example, we can see that there are 2 clean rooms which are occupied, none are due to depart, 26 are vacant, and none are blocked.

When it comes to our dirty rooms, 57 are occupied, none are due to depart, 74 are vacant, and none are blocked.

Rooms marked Dirty / Occupied will likely be guests who have already stayed overnight.

Vacant / Dirty rooms will be rooms that have been vacated, but not yet cleaned.

Departures

Much like the Arrivals section, the Departure module shows all the rooms expected to check out today.

In this examle, we can see that there were 56 check outs expected today.

The actual check out number is also 56, meaning that all of our expected check-outs have already departed.

The Due to Depart section shows 0, meaning there are no more check outs for the day.

VIP / Rewards Guest

The VIP / Rewards Guest element shows our arriving VIPs and GC Rewards members.

As we can see from this example, we are expecting 3 VIP Guests.

1 VIP Guest has already arrived.

We're expecting 69 GC Rewards guests.

20 of our expected GC Rewards guests have already arrived.

Live Status

This section gives us an overview of what's happening in the venue right at this moment.

Here we can see that there are currently 59 guests in house.

There are 108 reservations in the system for today.

Our current occupancy percentage is 36.88%, including Out of Order rooms.

Not including Out of Order rooms, our occupancy is still at 36.88%.

Forecast

The forecast section isn't necessarily relevant to you, but it's good to understand what it means.

Today, there are 108 rooms booked.

We're expecting 175 adults, and 13 children in house.

There will be 52 rooms available after all guests check in.

It's expected that we will make a total of $14,174.97 from today's bookings.

Our occupancy - including pending arrivals - is 67.5%.

That percentage excluding pending arrivals is still 67.5%

On average, our rooms today are priced at $131.25

History & Forecast

Like the Forecast, the History & Forecast section is not really relevant to you, but is still useful to know.

This chart shows the Average Room Rate, RevPAR (Revenue Per Available Room), and Occupancy for the current month.

The Average Room Rate is shown in red. On the 31st of July, 2024 the Average Room Rate was $139.58.

The RevPAR is in orange. It was 57.58 at the end of July in 2024.

The Occupancy percentage is in blue. The average occupancy percentage for 31/07/2024 was 41.25%.

Notifications

The Notifications section is where the tasks for the day will be displayed.

These tasks will need to be addressed by the relevant department as requested in the task.

Market Segments

This chart shows which market segment is most prevalent today.

In our example, the bulk of our guests today are under the Int Leis segment.

Folios under this segment are spending a total of $7,455.60.

As mentioned in the Forecast section, our total revenue for the day is $14,174.97.

Guest Requests

Any requests made by guests will show up in this section.

As with the Notifications, these will need to be actioned as required.

Client Management Tab

The Client Management tab is where we can view, edit and merge both guest and company profiles.

To view & edit a profile, select guest or company, depending on what you need.

Search the required name and double-click to open the profile.

Make any required changes, then save & exit the profile.

To merge profiles, first search for the required name.

Select the Main Guest, then the Other Guest profiles that need to be merged.

Click Merge.

This should consolidate all of the other profiles into the selected Main Guest profile.

Booking Tab Part 1

The Booking tab is where reservations are made and modified.

Each selection allows the user to perform a different task based in the reservations process.

Both Create Reservation & Quick Reservation allow you to make a new booking.

Manage Folio gives you a quick overview of details for each folio, and lets you quickly open and alter bookings.

Manage Reservation & Manage Reservation (By Page) do much the same as Manage Folio, though with less details immediately available at a glance.

Manage Suspended Reservation does ... (NO IDEA)

Booking Tab Part 2

Manage Tasks allows the user to add and edit Tasks.

On this page, you can see all of the current tasks, whether they are open/closed and who needs to action which Task.

The Groups section is much like the Reservation options, but for Group bookings instead of singular guests.

The Reservations Department will utilise these functions; you will likely not need this section at all.

Create Group Reservation is fairly self-explanatory. This option allows you to create a reservation for a group of people, such as a tour group.

Manage Group Reservation allows you to quickly and easily manage a booking for a group.

Manage Master Allotment lets you manage the rooms allotted to the master folio of a group.

Manage Sub Allotment allows you to manage the sub-allotments of a group folio.

Front Desk Tab Part 1

The Front Desk tab is going to be the main tab you will utilise as a GSA.

The Arrivals option allows you to quickly view all of the arrivals for the day.

In House lets you view all of the guests that are currently checked in.

Departures is where you will find the expected departures for the day.

Manage House Account is where the current Virtual Folios can be viewed.

If a guest has requested an Express Check Out, you will find it in this page.

Recent Check In/Out shows the most recent check ins and check outs in the system.

Front Desk Tab Part 2

The Front Desk Workspace dropdown allows for batch room assignment, charge and payment posting, as well as some extra options for keys. WILL THIS BE OUR NEW KEY CUTTING SYSTEM???

Batching allows us to quickly allocate rooms, post charges and payments to a collection of folios.

For example, on the Batch Room Assignment page you can type the allocated rooms numbers into the rooms that are currently unassigned, and allocate them all at once.

Similarly, charges and payments can be batched in their respective pages and posted all at once.

Guest Requests lets you quickly add any request a guest makes into the system. It will display in the Guest Request (Summary View) section of the home page.

Issue Additional / Replacement Key will let you make a note that extra (or replacement) keys have been cut for the room.
